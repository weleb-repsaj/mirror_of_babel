The Library of Babylon is a project spearheaded by Jonathen Basile. It was largely inspired by the short story "The Total Library" written by Jorge Luis Borges in 1939. In it, Borges describes an infinite library filled with enough books to write every possible combination of letters/symbols in existence. Basile's Library of Babel is an online adaptation of this idea. Visitors can either look through page after page of generated random letters or search for specific phrases or entire short stories.tional Recently, Basile and his colleagues have added an addition to the library: Babelia, a project that seeks to do what the original project did with characters, only with pixels. In this new project, visitors can find any image in the place where it has always existed, as far as the library is concerned.

Everything ever imaginable, ever Photoshoppable, ever writable, is already here in this sea of infinite RGB colors and letters. Nothing new can be created, only found. It is like staring into a mirror fixed to the inside of your forehead. Funnily enough, Borges himself wrote about the presence of a mirror in his short story. He writes, "[People] usually infer from this mirror that the Library is not infinite (if it were, why this illusory duplication?); I prefer to dream that its polished surfaces represent and promise the infinite"

Some have credited Borges with inventing the concept of the internet; others deny this. Because the Library of Babel is connected to the internet, this idea has come full circle. Every page of this imaginary library is now concretely accessible with a link.

It is remarkably difficult to find anything of value while randomly searching through the library's texts and images. I wanted to hack this randomness into order by curating a shelf myself, clearing it and then filling it with premonitions that have always existed but never together.

Sometimes existing on the internet can make you feel like a ghost, disembodied, floating. Borges said it would feel like this to exist in the library, too: "The certitude that everything has been written negates us or turns us into phantoms." I want to hack away this feeling with choice and creation, making a small enclave of the internet/library my own.
